How To Support Women in Cinema


One easy way to support women in cinema is choosing to watch movies with female directors, or films that focus on a main female character. Additionally, one easy way to figue out if a film cares about its female characters(and chooses to develop them as a person instaed of a love interest) is through the Bechdel Test.

The Bechdel Test was created by Alison Bechdel in response to female characters solely focusing on the actions of a man. In order for a film, or any work of art (comic, novel, etc.) to pass the Bechdel Test, two or more named female characters must talk about something other than a male character.
"The test is a blunt, basic measure of gender equality in a given film/show/book/etc. It revels in its own absurd simplicity. And it is often—still, ridiculously—not passed by Hollywood movies." (The Atlantic)
